Proof of Heart - Open Bitcoin Charity Platform

Proof of Heart is built on a simple idea: bitcoin donations should flow directly from donors to charities, using open infrastructure instead of closed, custodial platforms. This page explains why the Proof of Heart bitcoin charity platform exists.

Explore listed charities or read the full Proof of Heart paper.

The Problem with Traditional Donation Platforms

Traditional online donation platforms sit between donors and charities. They hold funds, charge fees, restrict regions, and sometimes block recipients entirely. For many non-profits, especially in sensitive environments, this is fragile and limiting.

Bitcoin offers an alternative: anyone can send value directly to anyone else. But for bitcoin donations to work well for non-profits, they need:

  • Clear, trustworthy information about where to donate bitcoin.
  • Simple ways to discover bitcoin charities and non-profits.
  • Infrastructure that never takes custody of funds.

A Non-Custodial Bitcoin Charity Platform

Proof of Heart focuses on publishing information, not holding money. Charities create a profile and share their bitcoin and lightning donation endpoints. Donors use their own wallets to support the charity directly.

  • No internal balances on the platform.
  • No custody of donor funds by Proof of Heart.
  • Charities remain responsible for their wallets and keys.

This non-custodial model aligns with Bitcoin's core principles: self-custody, openness, and resistance to censorship.

Open Infrastructure for Bitcoin Donations

Proof of Heart is meant to be open infrastructure rather than a closed, gatekept product. It aims to provide:

  • A neutral way to describe bitcoin and lightning donation endpoints.
  • Public, machine-readable profiles that other tools can build on.
  • A shared index of charities that accept bitcoin donations.

Wallets, explorers, and grassroots campaigns can point to these profiles when they want to highlight bitcoin charities and non-profits.

Principles Behind Proof of Heart

  • Open by default: Profiles and endpoints are publicly visible.
  • Non-custodial: The platform never holds donor funds.
  • Permissionless: Any eligible charity can publish a profile.
  • Interoperable: Data can be reused by other tools in the ecosystem.

If you share these values and want to accept bitcoin donations, you can create a Proof of Heart profile for your organization.
